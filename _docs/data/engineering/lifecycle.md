Data Engineering Lifecycle

Data Engineering gets Data, Stores it and Prepares it for consumption

Data Engineering is a discipline of interoperation - the connection of various technologies to serve business goals

Data Engineering Lifecycle involves:
- Generation
- Storage
- Ingestion
- Transformation
- Serving


Data Engineering Lifecycle requires:
- Security
- Data Management
- DataOps
- Data Architecture
- Orchestration
- Software Engineering


Cloud-first, managed-services in the cloud

Infrastructure and Systems are Ephemeral and Scalable

nb developing familiarity with Corporate Data Systems outside of a Corporate Environment remains difficult, creating barriers to entry

History highlights:
- Revolution 2000s-2010s: transition from batch to stream
- Revolution Hadoop ecosystem (Hadoop, HDFS, MapReduce, YARN, Spark): ascendance of MapReduce


cf Matt Turck' Data Landscape: 2012 vs 2021

Shift in focus, from who has the 'biggest data' to concerns over data management, data governance, data discovery and data quality improvements

an emphasis on decentralization and agility

Data Engineering sits upstream from Data Science and Analytics
cf Monica Rogati: The Data Science Hierarchy of Needs: (highest) AI/Deep Learning -> Learn/Optimize -> Aggregate/Label -> Move/Store -> Collect (lowest)
    Enterprises, she says, should build a solid data foundation (bottom 3 layers)


A DE optimizes along the axes of cost, agility, scalability, simplicity, reuse and interoperability

Data maturity: progression toward higher data utilization, capabilities and integration across the org - the way data is levraged as a competitive advantage
